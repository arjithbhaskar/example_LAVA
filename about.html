Est. 1963 · Six Decades of Stewardship

About LAVA

The Los Altos Village Association exists for one reason: to keep Downtown Los Altos the kind of place neighbors want to walk through, businesses want to open in, and families want to come back to.

Our Story

A village built on showing up.

Downtown Los Altos offers an historic, small-town feel where you can find sidewalk cafes, coffee shops, boutiques, vintage shops, and some of the best fine dining in the San Francisco Bay Area. With more than 150 retail, dining, service and professional businesses, Downtown Los Altos gives you that personal service only a small business community can provide.

Since 1963, LAVA has been the premier merchants' organization serving the Downtown Los Altos business community. With the primary goal of promoting downtown awareness and increasing foot traffic, we support businesses and the community through events, promotional advertising, beautification programs, and more.

"Together we can work towards strengthening and growing the Downtown community for generations to come."

The work isn't glamorous. It's tree lights and flower pots, permits and parking signs, sponsorships and sound checks. It's the third Wednesday of every month at 8:30 a.m., a board of merchants and residents around a table, deciding what this place becomes next.

What we do

LAVA presents more than three dozen fun, family-friendly events every year in Downtown Los Altos — including the Easter Egg Party, Arts & Wine Festival, Farmers' Market, Beer/Wine/Bubbly Strolls, Holiday Stroll, and Holiday Tree Lighting Ceremony. The organization also underwrites the annual Corvette Spectacular, year-round street tree lighting, maintenance of the flowerpots on Main and State Streets, and promotional marketing for the Downtown Los Altos triangle.

Milestones

1963

LAVA is founded

A handful of downtown merchants pool resources to promote the district.

1979

The first Arts & Wine Festival

What's now a 200-artist summer tradition begins as a modest weekend gathering.

1990s

Year-round street tree lighting

LAVA assumes ongoing responsibility for the lights that define the downtown evenings.

2010s

The Los Altos Village Fund

A 501(c)(3) charitable fund is established with the Los Altos Community Foundation to support LAVA's work.

Today

150+ members, 36+ events a year

Across six walkable blocks, LAVA continues to steward Downtown Los Altos for the next generation.

Our Mission

Promote. Steward. Gather.

The mission of the Los Altos Village Association is to promote and steward a vibrant Downtown Los Altos through civic engagement, local partnerships, strategic marketing, beautification, and events that support local businesses and bring the community together.

Board meetings
The LAVA Board of Directors meets the third Wednesday of every month at 8:30 a.m. Contact the LAVA office to find out how to join the meeting.

  • 01

    Civic Engagement

    Open board meetings, active partnership with city government, and a seat at every table where the future of Downtown is decided.

  • 02

    Local Partnerships

    Working hand-in-hand with the Los Altos Community Foundation, the city, our member businesses, and our neighbors.

  • 03

    Beautification

    Year-round street tree lighting and the seasonal flower pots on Main and State — maintained by us, enjoyed by everyone.

  • 04

    Events That Gather

    Family-friendly events that draw foot traffic, support our member businesses, and give the community reasons to come downtown.

  • 05

    Strategic Marketing

    Promoting Downtown Los Altos through advertising, social media, and the everyday word of mouth that small towns run on.
